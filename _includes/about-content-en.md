I hold a PhD in Computer Science from the Federal University of Pará (UFPA) and work at Brazil’s National Education and Research Network (RNP). My work sits at the intersection of computer networks, blockchain, and computational intelligence, with a particular interest in how these technologies can support distributed systems that are more secure, adaptable, and collaborative.

I also teach Computing. I enjoy experimenting with ways to make technical concepts more accessible — sometimes through software, sometimes by creating educational materials and activities such as the [Operational Stories](/projects/2025-historias-operacionais/) project.

## Research and background

My research interests include dynamic spectrum sharing, 5G and beyond networks, permissioned blockchain architectures, multi-agent systems, and federated learning.

My academic path began with a technical degree in Telecommunications at the Federal Institute of Pará. I later completed a bachelor’s, a master’s, and a PhD in Computer Science at UFPA.
